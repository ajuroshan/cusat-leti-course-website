Academic Programs

Master's Degree Program "New generation of electronic component base"

The master's program focuses on education of specialists in the field of development and creation of electronic component base elements based on both traditional semiconductor technologies and new principles, including elements of radio photonics, semiconductor heterostructures and high bandgap semiconductors.

Key Details:

  • Program Code: 11.04.04. Electronics and nanoelectronics
  • Language: English
  • Duration: 2 years (4 semesters)
  • Credits: 120 ECTS

Curriculum Highlights:

  • Computer Technology and Simulation in Electronics
  • Micro- and Nanotechnology Processes
  • Semiconductor Heterostructure Technology
  • Integral and Fibre Optics
  • Computer Simulation and Design of Microwave and Optical Electronics Instruments and Devices

Research Laboratories:

  • Laboratory of Microwave Photonics and Magnonics (LMPM)
  • Laboratory of Solar Heterostructural Photopower
  • Resource Center "Solid State Physics and Materials Science for Radioelectronic and Telecommunication Systems"

Focus of Interest:

  • Basic technological routes and parameters of technological processes of production microelectronic products;
  • Basics of material science of semiconductors and heterostructures;
  • Design of devices, appliances and systems of electronic technics taking into account the given requirements;

Ph.D. Research: Graduates can continue their studies at the post-graduate level in various programs including Physics and Astronomy, Electronics, Radio Engineering and Communication Systems, Photonics, instrumentation, optical and biotechnical systems, and technologies.

Saint Petersburg Electrotechnical University "LETI"

About LETI

The university was founded in 1886 by Emperor Alexander III and has maintained cooperation with SIEMENS since 1897. It's the oldest higher educational institution of electrical engineering in Europe.

Historical Achievements
  • Inventor of Radio Communication: Prof. Alexander S. Popov (1859-1906)
  • Nobel Prize Laureate in Physics (2000): Prof. Zhores I. Alferov
